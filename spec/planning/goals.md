Goal 1 — IR + Code distillation, fused, on one slice (OFBiz tax-exemption). These co-evolve, so keep them in one goal where the feedback is instant. The blind-test exit criterion is the forcing function: when generated tests can't express a behavior, that's the IR schema telling you it's missing a claim type. Let the goal discover the IR by trying to distill real code and hitting walls. An IR developed in isolation first is a spec written before you've met the problem — you'd design claim types for behaviors you imagine OFBiz has, then find the real tax code needs three you didn't foresee. Fuse them and the IR earns its shape empirically. This is your funded spike; everything else is downstream of it existing.
Goal 2 — PR-history distillation (detailed spec: Airlift-goal-2-pr-history.md). Genuinely separable, and correctly sequenced after, because it consumes the IR as a fixed input. Your "multilevel blame" maps commits to claims, and claims are IR objects — so this goal can't even define "done" until Goal 1 has frozen a claim schema. Run it second, treat the IR as a stable contract, and if PR-history reveals an IR gap, that's a versioned change to a shipped artifact, not a live renegotiation. Different regime entirely.
Goal 3 — Architecture distillation (detailed spec: Airlift-goal-3-architecture.md). The most independent of the four — different source of truth (patterns/ADRs, not business rules), different output format (mustache pattern library), different validation (a swapped architecture leaves behavior invariant — your own dividing line). It shares almost no surface with the biz-logic track. Run it whenever; it doesn't block and isn't blocked.
Goal 4 - Airlift MCP. Make Airlift IR available for coding agents and harnesses such as OpenAI Symphony. (Detailed spec: Airlift-goal-4-mcp.md)
Goal 5 - Drift detection and compensation. (Detailed spec: Airlift-goal-5-drift.md)
Goal 6 - Glossary. The cornerstone stated in the whitepaper: the glossary is the lingua franca between AI and humans AND the bridge between Airlift and the org's other knowledge bases — foremost Airbase (our per-team specialization of the Team Brain KB product; it consolidates tribal knowledge, wikis, PM docs, and ongoing meetings). Elevate the glossary from a per-target IR artifact to a federated, governed, org-bridging layer: namespacing across targets/teams, term linter, alias mapping to Airbase concepts. (Detailed spec: Airlift-goal-6-glossary.md)
Goal 7 - Productionalize Airlift. Org reality: 100+ microservices, half a dozen teams, hundreds of thousands of lines of code, Conway-partitioned. Centralized persistent storage + MCP, partitioned per team; process and features to cooperate with other KBs (responsibility split: Airlift is un-opinionated and fact-driven from source code and commit history — semantics and trajectory, no opinion on direction; Airbase works with engineers, capturing and distilling intent; Airlift grounds Airbase and helps execute Airbase's vision); cooperation with Maestro (the Symphony-clone PR-lifetime harness); proactive drift-fighting at scale; and the carrot — immediate benefit for teams that onboard. (Detailed spec: Airlift-goal-7-productionalization.md)
